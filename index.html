The Problem

Most Amazon search results are noise

A large percentage of third-party sellers on Amazon and Walmart use randomly generated names like XKJRUIE, TUYUUOR, and BFGQWZ. These sellers typically list low-quality products with misleading photos and manufactured reviews. They make it significantly harder to find legitimate products.

Detection

Scoring is based on multiple signals

Each seller is evaluated against several criteria: name entropy (gibberish detection), account age, review distribution patterns, and listing quality indicators. Sellers that fall below the threshold are flagged. A badge counter on the extension icon shows the number of filtered listings per page.

Verified Sellers

Trusted sellers are labeled with reasoning

Sellers that score above the trust threshold receive a verified badge directly on the listing. Clicking the badge shows the specific criteria that contributed to the score: account age, review patterns, brand registration status, and community report history. If a badge appears incorrect, users can submit a dispute for manual review.

Reporting

User reports go through manual review

Users can report sellers directly from any listing. Reports are queued for manual review before any action is taken. This prevents abuse and protects legitimate sellers from false flags. Confirmed reports propagate to the shared blocklist automatically.

Configuration

Filter behavior is user-configurable

Three filter modes: badge only, dim, or hide completely. Sensitivity is adjustable. Users can maintain a personal whitelist to override the filter for specific sellers, and a personal blocklist to force-filter sellers regardless of their score.

Privacy

Detection is client-side. No data leaves your browser.

Seller analysis runs entirely in the extension. No browsing history, search queries, or purchase data is collected or transmitted. The only network requests are anonymous blocklist syncs and user-initiated reports. No account system exists. There are no ads.
